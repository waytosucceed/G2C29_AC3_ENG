What did I learn?

Verbs are a group of words that show action or events performed by a person. They tell about things that happen or the state of doing some activities.

An action verb describes the action that the main character of the sentence performs.

Here's a quick reminder of some action verbs we've seen:

  • Run - like Felix running through the forest
  • Jump - like hopping over big rocks
  • Play - having fun with friends
  • Discover - finding something new and exciting
  • Climb - going up mountains or trees
  • Swim - moving through water
  • Dance - moving your body to music
  • Laugh - making happy sounds

But guess what? There are so many more action verbs in our world! Think about:

  • Eat (when you munch on your favourite snack)
  • Write (when you're doing your homework)
  • Sing (when you're in the shower or with friends)
  • Build (when you're playing with blocks)
  • Read (when you're enjoying a good book)
